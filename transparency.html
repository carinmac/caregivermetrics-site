Transparency Document

What we collect, what we do with it, and what you get back.

Caregiver Metrics is not a clinical service. Nothing in your report constitutes medical or psychological advice. If you are in distress, please reach out to a qualified professional.

What you log
Each time you record an event in the app, you provide:
Your nameFirst name only, used to identify your entries
What happenedA free-text description in your own words. The most valuable field in the app.
DateWhen the event occurred
Care roleThe category of caregiving task
Time spentHours and minutes
Three caregiving questionsWas this hard to do? Did something outside your control make it harder? How comfortable are you with this type of task now?
Nine emotion ratingsHow intensely you felt each of nine emotions, rated 1 to 10
Out of pocket costAny direct expenses incurred, if applicable
What happens to your entries
Once a week, I review everything you logged.

I score each entry using a framework I developed. The scores look at three things: how complex the care itself was, how much the system made things harder than they needed to be, and how much skill and expertise you applied.

These scores are not visible in the app. They exist in a separate workbook that only I can access. They are what makes your weekly report specific to you, not generic.

When the system created most of the burden, your report will say that plainly. When your skill and judgment were the reason something worked, your report will say that too.

What you get back
Every week you receive a personal report written specifically for your data.

The report is not automated. It is written by me. Each report includes a summary of your caregiving activity, the unpaid professional value of your hours, an observation about where your burden came from, and what your emotion data showed.

Your report will also reflect something most caregivers never hear. You are not just surviving this. You are building real expertise. Reading deterioration before professionals notice. Making impossible decisions with incomplete information. Holding a care team together when the system will not. The judgment, the advocacy, the pattern recognition. Your data tracks those too. They are real. They are yours.

From a sample weekly report

"Your highest burden event this week carried real clinical weight. Wound care is skilled work, and you were monitoring closely enough to know something needed attention. What made it worse was a system that did not answer the phone. Three calls, no response, an unplanned two and a half hour visit that you absorbed entirely. The complexity was real. The system failure on top of it was not your fault."

Your personal dashboard
Your data is also visualized in a personal dashboard that updates automatically as you log.

The dashboard shows your time spent by care role, events logged over time, emotional patterns, and the financial value of your unpaid work.

Your personal dashboard. Updates automatically each time you log an entry.
Why this beta exists
This beta exists to prove that measuring the weight of care does not just name it. It starts to move it.

When caregivers can see themselves clearly, their time, their load, their patterns, that visibility changes things. Not just for them. For everyone who should have been paying attention. What gets measured gets seen. What gets seen can be shared, shifted, and carried differently.

The version you are using today is intentionally simple. You will notice missing features. Voice input, camera, automatic receipt capture, instant document export. Those are coming. What we need first is proof that the data itself is meaningful, that the framework works, and that caregivers find value in seeing their experience reflected back.

That proof is you. We are not there yet. And this is where it starts.

What I do not do

I will never share your entries with anyone else. Not other beta users, not researchers, not third parties.

I will never use your data to make clinical assessments or provide medical or psychological advice.

I will never contact you based on your emotion scores without your consent.

I will never sell, license, or distribute your data in any form.

Your data and privacy

Your entries are visible only to you and me. No other beta user can see your data.

Your data is stored on Google's secure cloud infrastructure. We chose this platform specifically because your entries never pass through third-party servers.

Your name is used only to identify your entries during analysis. It does not appear in any aggregate reporting.

You may request deletion of your data at any time by emailing caregivermetrics@gmail.com.

Your participation is governed by the confidentiality agreement you signed before accessing the app.

Questions about how your data is used? Email us before you start logging: caregivermetrics@gmail.com
